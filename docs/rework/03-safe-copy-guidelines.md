# Merlin Safe and Credible Copy Guidelines

## 1. Objective

Create language that is insightful, grounded, and psychologically safe.

The tone should support reflection and agency, not fear or certainty.

## 2. Voice and Framing

Use:
- Probabilistic statements
- Time-window language
- User agency and decision support framing
- Grounding and external verification reminders

Avoid:
- Absolute predictions
- Catastrophic framing
- Enemy/paranoia framing
- Medical, legal, or financial certainty claims

## 3. Phrase Rules

Allowed patterns:
- "This period may increase..."
- "You might notice..."
- "Potential pressure is elevated in..."
- "Consider pausing before major conclusions."

Blocked patterns:
- "This will happen..."
- "You are definitely entering..."
- "Someone is plotting against you..."
- "You cannot avoid..."

## 4. Message Structure Template

For every major forecast card:
1. Signal summary
2. Why this signal is elevated
3. Domain impact possibilities
4. Agency action steps
5. Uncertainty reminder
6. Grounding suggestion

Example template:
- Signal: "Relational pressure is elevated this week."
- Why: "Two exact aspects are increasing emotional reactivity and communication friction."
- Impact: "Conversations may feel charged, especially around boundaries."
- Agency: "Use written clarification before high-stakes discussions."
- Uncertainty: "Interpretation is directional, not a guarantee."
- Grounding: "Pause and verify assumptions with direct evidence."

## 5. Domain-Specific Copy Patterns

Identity:
- "Identity questions may feel louder than usual."
- "Try small experiments instead of irreversible moves."

Career:
- "Decision pressure may rise around priorities and accountability."
- "Sequence large decisions after fact-checking and peer review."

Relationships:
- "Relational sensitivity may be elevated in this window."
- "Clarify intent before interpreting tone."

Finances:
- "Risk perception may feel amplified this week."
- "Delay major commitments until a second review is complete."

Mental strain:
- "Cognitive load may run high in this period."
- "Reduce optional complexity and preserve recovery blocks."

## 6. High-Intensity Safety Inserts

Trigger condition:
- Any domain pressure >= 75

Required insertions:
- Grounding prompt
- External verification prompt
- Delay-major-conclusion cue

Approved insert examples:
- "When intensity is high, assumptions can feel more certain than they are. Verify externally before acting."
- "If this feels overwhelming, reduce input and revisit tomorrow with fresh context."

## 7. Explainability Copy Requirements

Every score explanation must include:
- Top 3 factors
- Relative contribution language
- Personalization modifier note
- Confidence qualifier

Good example:
- "This score is mainly driven by a tightening Saturn aspect, a stacked communication transit, and a house activation match. Your profile increases sensitivity in relational contexts, so this domain is weighted slightly higher. Confidence is moderate due to limited check-in history."

## 8. Do and Do Not Examples

Do:
- "Pressure is elevated; choose reversible steps."
- "Use this as a reflection signal, not a fixed verdict."

Do not:
- "A betrayal is imminent."
- "You are under attack."
- "Failure is unavoidable this month."

## 9. Compliance Checklist for Content Reviews

1. No deterministic certainty claims
2. No fear amplification or paranoia cues
3. Includes agency recommendation
4. Includes uncertainty language
5. Includes grounding prompt at high intensity
6. Explainability language present for major scores

## 10. UI Microcopy Snippets

Tooltip: confidence
- "Confidence reflects signal clarity and data completeness, not certainty."

Tooltip: personalization
- "Personalization adjusts weighting based on your profile and reflection history."

CTA: check-in
- "Log your experience to improve calibration over time."

CTA: grounding
- "Pause, verify, and choose the smallest next step."

## 11. Escalation and Safeguard Note

When user text indicates crisis, panic, or harmful ideation:
- Stop predictive framing
- Switch to grounding and support-oriented language
- Provide non-interpretive, stabilizing prompts

Product note:
- Merlin is a reflective timing tool, not a diagnostic or emergency service.
